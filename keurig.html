Autopsy Report

Keurig Kold

Time of Death: June 2016

Background Check

Keurig launched the Kold machine in 2015 as its ambitious entry into the cold beverage market. The countertop system promised to dispense Coca-Cola, Dr Pepper, and other chilled drinks at the push of a button, much like its hot coffee brewers. Backed by a partnership with Coca-Cola and significant marketing investment, Keurig Kold aimed to disrupt the soda industry by bringing it into people’s homes.

Cause of Death

Despite a high-profile launch, Keurig Kold flopped within a year. Consumers were put off by the $369 price tag and the bulky, 23-pound design. Each drink pod cost significantly more than a canned soda, and the machine took minutes to chill a beverage, undermining its convenience. Poor sales led Keurig to discontinue the product in June 2016 and issue full refunds to customers.

Underlying Conditions

  • Excessive price — the machine retailed at $369, far more than consumers were willing to pay.
  • Inefficient performance — drinks took minutes to chill, slower than simply using a fridge.
  • Poor value — pods were more expensive than buying soda cans or bottles at the store.
  • Bulky design — heavy, noisy, and impractical for most kitchen counters.

Autopsy Findings

  • Even strong brand partnerships cannot overcome weak product-market fit.
  • Consumers will not adopt new appliances unless they provide clear convenience or savings.
  • Pricing must align with perceived value, especially against easy alternatives.

Preventative Measures

  • Conduct deeper consumer testing before large-scale rollout to validate demand.
  • Balance innovation with practicality — ensure new tech improves daily life in a meaningful way.
  • Adopt phased launches with prototypes to avoid over-investment in unproven concepts.
