Autopsy Report

Quibi

Time of Death: October 2020

Artifact

Background Check

Quibi was founded in 2018 by Jeffrey Katzenberg and led by CEO Meg Whitman. It launched in April 2020 as a short-form streaming platform designed specifically for mobile devices, offering episodes that were generally under 10 minutes, branded as “quick bites” creating a new way to tell stories for people on the go. The platform aimed to capture viewers on-the-go by delivering content in brief, easily consumable segments, positioning itself as an alternative to traditional streaming services.

What Happened

Quibi struggled to attract subscribers, reaching only a fraction of projected users. Launching during the COVID-19 pandemic reduced demand for mobile-only content, and a lack of compelling exclusive shows and inability to watch on TVs contributed to its failure. The platform shut down in October 2020, just six months after launch.

Root Causes

  • Mobile-only viewing: Restricting content to smartphones limited audience reach and prevented adoption among users who preferred TVs or tablets.
  • Weak content library: The platform lacked strong, exclusive shows that could attract and retain subscribers in a competitive streaming market.
  • Poor timing: Launching during the COVID-19 pandemic reduced demand for mobile-focused, on-the-go entertainment, as most potential users were staying at home.
  • Late market entry: Quibi entered an already saturated streaming market dominated by established players, making it difficult to differentiate and capture attention.

Mitigations

  • Evaluate product-market fit and consumer behavior to ensure the platform’s content format and viewing model match real user needs and habits, avoiding mismatches between the product and market readiness.
  • Invest in exclusive, high-quality content as a core value proposition that differentiates the platform from competitors and drives consumer adoption.
  • Offer flexible viewing options across mobile and TV devices to expand the addressable market and increase customer convenience, aligning distribution with actual customer preferences.

Autopsy Findings

  • Understanding consumer habits and platform preferences is crucial, as products that do not align with how and when users want to consume content are unlikely to gain traction.
  • Content must be compelling and distinctive enough to compete in saturated markets, providing a strong value proposition that convinces users to switch or subscribe.
  • Timing and broader market conditions can significantly impact adoption, meaning even innovative products may fail if launched when the market is not ready or external factors reduce demand.
