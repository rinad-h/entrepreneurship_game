Autopsy Report

Quibi

Time of Death: October 2020

Background Check

Quibi was founded in 2018 by Jeffrey Katzenberg and led by CEO Meg Whitman. It launched in April 2020 as a short-form streaming platform designed for mobile devices, offering episodes under 10 minutes called "quick bites." Quibi aimed to compete with established streaming services by targeting users on-the-go.

Cause of Death

Quibi struggled to attract subscribers, reaching only a fraction of projected users. Launching during the COVID-19 pandemic reduced demand for mobile-only content, and a lack of compelling exclusive shows and inability to watch on TVs contributed to its failure. The platform shut down in October 2020, just six months after launch.

Underlying Conditions

  • Mobile-only viewing limited audience reach.
  • Content library lacked strong, exclusive shows to attract subscribers.
  • Pandemic reduced demand for mobile-focused entertainment.
  • Late entry into an already saturated streaming market.

Autopsy Findings

  • Understanding consumer habits and platform preferences is crucial.
  • Content must be compelling enough to compete in saturated markets.
  • Timing and market conditions can significantly impact adoption.

Preventative Measures

  • Conduct in-depth market research to validate content format and device preferences.
  • Focus on exclusive, high-quality content to differentiate from competitors.
  • Allow flexible viewing options, including mobile and TV devices.
