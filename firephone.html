Autopsy Report

Amazon Fire Phone

Time of Death: August 2015

Background Check

The Amazon Fire Phone was launched in July 2014 as Amazon’s first smartphone, developed under Jeff Bezos and the Amazon Devices team. It was designed to tie users deeper into Amazon’s ecosystem, including Prime, shopping, and media services. However, the phone struggled to compete in a saturated smartphone market.

Cause of Death

The Fire Phone was discontinued in August 2015, just over a year after its launch. Poor sales stemmed from its high price, lack of standout features compared to Apple and Samsung devices, and a weak app ecosystem. Amazon wrote down $170 million in unsold inventory.

Underlying Conditions

  • High price point with limited value compared to established competitors.
  • Lack of compelling or unique features to attract customers.
  • Weak app ecosystem, making the phone less appealing to users.
  • Overreliance on tying customers to Amazon’s ecosystem instead of broad usability.

Autopsy Findings

  • Competing in the smartphone market requires standout features and strong app support.
  • Pricing must align with consumer expectations and perceived value.
  • Relying solely on ecosystem lock-in is not enough to win new customers.

Preventative Measures

  • Conduct deeper market testing before entering highly competitive industries.
  • Focus on differentiating features that provide real user benefits.
  • Build stronger partnerships with app developers to support the ecosystem.
