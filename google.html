Autopsy Report

Google Glass

Time of Death: March 2023

Background Check

Google Glass was launched in 2012 by Google X (founded by Sergey Brin and Larry Page) as a wearable smart glasses device. It promised hands-free access to information, navigation, and augmented reality. The product generated huge hype but faced public pushback and limited adoption before being discontinued for consumers in 2015, later pivoting to enterprise use.

What Happened

Despite early buzz, Glass failed in the consumer market. High pricing ($1,500), privacy concerns about the built-in camera, and limited real-world usefulness led to negative media coverage. Sales stagnated, developers lost interest, and Google officially ended the consumer edition in 2015, shifting the product to niche business applications.

Root Causes

  • High cost — too expensive for average consumers at $1,500.
  • Privacy concerns — constant recording raised fears of surveillance.
  • Lack of clear use case — unclear value beyond novelty led to weak adoption.

Lessons

  • Innovative technology must solve a clear, everyday problem to succeed.
  • Public perception and trust are as important as technical capability.

Preventative Measures

  • Validate real customer needs before mass release of experimental products.
  • Address ethical, privacy, and social concerns proactively during design and rollout.
